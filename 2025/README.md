## Main Themes

- Hybrid Threats
- Geopolitical Cybersecurity
- Cybercrime
- Cybersecurity Situation 2025
- Digital Sovereignty
- European Cloud Security
- Critical Infrastructure Protection
- Cyber Hygiene
- Artificial Intelligence Security
- Hybrid Warfare
- Disinformation and Foreign Influence Operations
- Government–Industry–Academia Collaboration

## Conference Statistics

- **Conference Duration:** 2 Days
- **Sessions:** 17
- **Keynotes:** 8
- **Panel Discussions:** 8
- **Spotlight Talks:** 1
- **Opening / Closing Sessions:** 2

## Major Topics Compared to SIKO 2024

- Stronger emphasis on **hybrid threats** and geopolitical cyber conflict.
- Growing focus on **European digital sovereignty** and secure cloud adoption.
- Introduction of dedicated discussions on **AI security** rather than only AI for cybersecurity.
- Increased attention to **disinformation**, foreign influence campaigns, and societal resilience.
- Continued focus on **critical infrastructure protection** and cyber resilience.
- Cyber hygiene recognized as a foundational requirement for national cybersecurity.
- Cloud security and technological sovereignty positioned as strategic priorities for Europe.
